The War Room

Welcome to the War Room - your world superpower simulator. 38 territories, a zoomable map, and accurate stats make the user experience out of this world. Each territory starts with one capital fortress, and players can build additional forts to conquer the world. Resources are produced daily by an empire and traded between players. When a capital fortress trades hands, so does the terriory, and the only way to win is to control the entire map.


Rule Your Empire


Claim your empire and rule it with undisputed power. Your strategy and success depend upon the geography and resources of your land. Once daily, each territory in your control produces resources. Throughout the day, some of these resources are consumed by the population to survive. To rule the world and win the game you must keep the balance of production and consumption in check.




Develop Your Territories


Build forts and camps around the world to hold resources, conduct trade, and execute battle. Encampments are cheap and temporary. They may be placed in any territory and their limited visibility promotes secret diplomacy. Fortresses are strongholds to defend territories and maintain power. They may be placed only in controlled territories and are visible to all players. The ability to effectively balance the two types of structures is what can make or break your conquest experience.




Trade With Allies


No territory is perfect. The scarcity of each resource varies across the map. Trade must be leveraged to build your empire and keep the population alive. Shipment comes at a cost and takes time to complete. The price of a shipment depends on the length of the route, the amount of goods being shipped, and the infrastructure of the territory in which goods are being shipped. Any two things can be traded - including information. If two players decide to trade secrets, resources can be "gifted" in a tactical, honor system agreement.




Battle With Enemies


For the game to end, all territories must be controlled by one player: the winner. In each game you will start with an empire that is comprised of adjacent territories. Only through battle may you expand your empire and dominate the world. Each territory starts with an army. Each game peice represents a certain number of soldiers, and when that peice is gone, so are those troops. By battling a fortress or encampment successfully you win control of that location. Ownership of the territory lies in ownership of the capital fortress.




Battle on Land


Play Battle on Land when fighting territories connected by land. It's chess; pretty self explanatory.




Battle at Sea


Play Battle at Sea when fighting across oceans. Place your ships on the grid, and fire random shots at the enemy to sink their fleat.




Conspire Offline


While the War Room is an effective diplomacy portal, we highly encourage you to conspire offline. The actions taken online are only as entertaining as the ideas they stand for, and the embodiment of those ideas is what can make the game experience fantastic. With means such as social media, word processors, and file sharing sites, you can take the politics of the War Room into the real spheres of the internet.
